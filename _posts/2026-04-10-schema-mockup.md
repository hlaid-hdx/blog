During my team's creation of our mockup and schema, I was able to provide my notes from our first client meeting to help organize our thoughts before we began building anything. Those notes helped us return to what the client actually said instead of relying only on memory or assumptions.

## My Role in the Mockup

The production of our mockup was largely a team effort. Each person had their own piece to add, and we worked through many of the decisions collaboratively through conversation. My main individual contribution was building out the initial landing page. I also consulted on the different views that other group members were building and helped talk through ideas about how the pages should connect to the client's goals.

Overall, I felt that I had at least some involvement in the ideas behind almost every part of the initial mockup. Even when I was not directly building a specific page, I was still participating in the conversation about how the page should work and how it would fit into the overall project.

## My Role in the Schema

When it came to the schema, my group selected Jay as the designated schema architect. I believe he took influence from our group's conversations about the back end, but I also want to be honest that the schema itself was mostly his creation. The rest of us, including me, tried to familiarize ourselves with it so that we could understand the design and be prepared to discuss it during presentations if needed.

This helped me realize that even when one person is mainly responsible for a technical part of the project, the whole group still needs to understand it. If the schema supports the client interactions, then everyone on the team needs to know how the data connects to the pages and user stories.

## Mockup and Schema Presentation Days

For the mockup and schema presentation days, I was selected to rotate through the other groups and see what they had currently laid out. I particularly enjoyed this because I was able to see the different methods and strategies other groups used to align their projects with their clients' goals.

Some groups had very concrete plans, while others had mockups and schemas that were less filled out and more flexible. At first, I thought some of those groups might have been behind in forming their vision. After having about a week of separation from the presentations, though, I feel differently. I now think that some groups were intentionally leaving room to clarify with their clients instead of assuming that one meeting gave them enough information to completely define the project.

That was a helpful realization for me. It reminded me that moving quickly is not always the same as moving correctly. Sometimes a flexible early design can be better if it leaves space for client feedback.

## Future Plans

Moving forward, I plan to continue verbally assisting my team when we are working through ideas, questions, or uncertainty about direction. I also look forward to using more artistic creativity in the landing page and helping it feel more polished and intentional.

One idea I am interested in exploring is finding a way to better use the existing SharePoint as a resource for the webpage rather than treating it as a relic of the past. If the client already has useful information there, then our project may be stronger if we find a way to organize or connect to that content instead of ignoring it.

I also want to take high-quality notes again during our second client meeting. I felt that my notes from the first meeting were useful, and I want to continue being an active voice in asking questions and clarifying the client's goals. My main goal for the next meeting is to better understand what the client wants from the project so that our team can make decisions based on their actual needs rather than our assumptions.

## Reflection

So far, this project has shown me how important communication is in a team setting. Building the mockup was not just about writing HTML or making pages look good. It was also about interpreting the client's needs, comparing ideas with teammates, and making sure the design choices were connected to the larger purpose of the project.

I feel good about the role I have played so far, especially in helping organize our early thoughts and contributing to the landing page. As we move forward, I want to become more involved in turning our ideas into a finished product and making sure our design choices continue to reflect the client's goals.
